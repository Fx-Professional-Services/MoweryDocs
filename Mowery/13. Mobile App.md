# Cancel a Dispatched Service Call

Use this procedure to cancel a dispatched service call and understand how it affects the FileMaker Go mobile app.

The **Service Call** screen is the first screen you see when you log in to the mobile app. It serves as your main workspace for viewing and managing service calls.

This guide walks you through the full workflow, from reviewing a service call to creating estimates, invoicing, and recording payments.

---

## Step 1: Review the Service Call

When the screen loads, the **Ticket Details** tab is selected by default.

Start by reviewing the key information:

### Check the Schedule

- **Schedule Date** – Confirms the service date.
    
- **Time Slot** – Indicates whether the job is in the AM or PM.
    

### Confirm Customer Details

- **Customer Name and ID** appear on the left panel.
    
- Review the **Contact Info** section for phone numbers.
    

### Verify the Service Location

- Check the **Property Address** to ensure you are going to the correct site.
    

---

## Step 2: Understand the Job

Scroll to the **Description** field and read the full job details carefully.

Look for:

- Type of service (e.g., tune-up, repair)
    
- Agreement details and expiration dates
    
- Special instructions
    

> **Tip:** Always review the Description before starting work to avoid missing details.

---

## Step 3: Review Service Information

Check the service configuration:

- **Service Department** – Identifies the assigned team
    
- **Service Type** – Defines the job category
    
- **Payment Type** – Indicates how the job will be paid (e.g., prepaid)
    

---

## Step 4: Check Additional Instructions

If available, review:

- **Directions To** – May include gate codes, parking instructions, or landmarks
    
- **Property Notes** – Internal comments from dispatch or staff
    

---

## Step 5: Navigate Between Tabs

Use the tabs at the top of the screen to access more information:

### Ticket Details (Default View)

- Contains all core job information
    

### Service Call History

- View previous service visits
    
- Helps understand past issues or recurring problems
    

### Equipment / Maintenance Agreements

- Check installed equipment
    
- Review active service agreements
    

### Estimate

- View or create job estimates (see Steps 8–10 below)
    

### Invoice

- Access billing details and payment records (see Steps 11–15 below)
    

---

## Step 6: Close the App

Tap **Close App** when you are done using the mobile app.

---

# Estimate → Invoice Workflow

After reviewing the service call, you can create an **Estimate** and convert it into an **Invoice**.

---

## Step 7: Open the Estimate Tab

1. Tap the **Estimate** tab at the top of the screen.
    
2. The screen displays any existing estimates for the service call.
    
---

## Step 8: Review or Create an Estimate

### If an Estimate Exists

- Review listed items and pricing.
    
- Confirm quantities and services match the work required.
    

### If No Estimate Exists

1. Add line items for:
    
    - Services performed
        
    - Parts used
        
2. Ensure all required items are included before proceeding.
    

> **Tip:** Verify accuracy before moving to invoicing.

---

## Step 9: Verify Estimate Details

Before converting the estimate:

- Confirm pricing and totals
    
- Check applicable taxes
    
- Ensure all services and materials are listed
    
- Review with the customer if required

# Estimate Form User Guide

## Purpose

The Estimate Form displays customer, service, equipment, and pricing information related to HVAC or plumbing service estimates. The system automatically populates most fields using customer records, equipment data, and selected service tasks.

---

# Header Section

## Company Information

The header displays the service provider’s information.

Information displayed includes:

- Company name
- Company address
- Contact numbers
- Website
- Social media information

### Purpose

This section identifies the company responsible for the estimate.

---

# Customer and Service Information

## Customer Information

The system displays customer contact information associated with the service record.

Displayed information includes:

- Customer name
- Work phone
- Home phone
- Mobile number
- Email address

### Purpose

This section provides customer contact details for service coordination and communication.

---

## Property Address

The system displays the address where the service will be performed.

### Purpose

This section identifies the service location.

---

## Billing Address

The system displays the billing address associated with the customer account.

### Purpose

This section identifies where invoices or billing documents are sent.

---

## Service Information

The Service Information section displays scheduling and estimate details.

Displayed information includes:

- Service type
- Estimated start date
- Estimated completion date
- Estimate date

### Purpose

This section provides service scheduling information.

---

# Inspection Checklist

The Inspection Checklist displays inspection and maintenance items related to HVAC components.

## Categories

- Compressor
- Condenser Coil
- Refrigerant
- Fan and Motor
- Evaporation Coil
- Condensate Areas
- Air Filters
- Heating Assembly
- Electrical Components
- Thermostat
- Accessories

---

## Checkbox Indicators

Checked boxes indicate completed inspections, verified conditions, or maintenance tasks.

### Examples

- Electrical Connections
- Fuel Supply & Pressure
- Overload
- RV Value

### Purpose

This section documents completed inspection and maintenance activities.

---

# Reason for Call

The Reason for Call section displays the purpose of the service visit.

### Example

> Tune up the gas furnace under the agreement.

### Purpose

This section provides context for the service request.

---

# Recommendations and Estimates

The Recommendations/Estimates section displays technician recommendations or follow-up actions.

### Example

> For consultation after 6 months

### Purpose

This section provides maintenance recommendations and future service guidance.

---

# Equipment Information

The Equipment Information table displays serviced equipment details.

## Table Columns

### Equipment Type

Displays the equipment category.

### Model

Displays the equipment model number.

### Serial

Displays the equipment serial number.

### Age

Displays the equipment age.

### Condition

Displays the equipment condition.

### Example

- Condition: Good

### Purpose

This section provides equipment identification and condition information.

---

# Task Code and Pricing

The pricing section displays service tasks and estimate details.

## Columns

### Task Code

Displays the internal task or service code.

### Description

Displays the service description.

### Standard Price

Displays the standard service price.

### Savings

Displays discounts or savings applied to the estimate.

### Purpose

This section provides a breakdown of estimated service costs.

---

# Tax and Total Estimate

The totals section appears at the bottom-right corner of the form.

## Tax

Displays the calculated tax amount.

### Example

- Tax: $780.00

---

## Total Estimate

Displays the final estimated amount.

### Example

- Total Estimate: $6,089.00

### Purpose

This section summarizes the estimated customer charges.

---

# Warranty and Labor Guarantee

This section displays warranty and labor guarantee information.

Displayed information may include:

- Parts warranty details
- Labor guarantee duration
- Repair limitations and conditions

### Purpose

This section defines service warranty coverage and repair terms.

---

# Authorization Section

The Authorization Section appears at the bottom of the form.

## Customer Signature

The customer signs this section to approve the estimate and authorize the listed repairs or services.

### Purpose

This section confirms customer authorization before work begins.
    

---

## Step 10: Convert Estimate to Invoice

1. Navigate to the **Invoice** tab.
    
2. Create or open the invoice linked to the estimate.
    
3. In most cases, the invoice will automatically include all items from the estimate.
    

---

## Step 11: Review the Invoice

- Confirm all estimate items are included.
    
- Verify:
    
    - Total amount
        
    - Taxes
        
    - Payment type
        

> **Tip:** Ensure everything matches the agreed work.

---

## Step 12: Record Payment

If payment is collected on-site:
## Steps

1. From the **Service Calls** list, select a dispatched call.
    
2. Click the **left arrow** to open the dispatched call.
    
3. Click the **dropdown menu**, then select **Cancel Call**.
    
4. Open **FileMaker Go** on your iPad.
    
5. Tap **Close File** once.
    
    - The FileMaker Go app exits completely.
        
    - Cancelled service calls no longer appear in the mobile app.
        
    - When the app is reopened after a cancelled service call, it may display a **blank screen** because there are no active service calls assigned.
