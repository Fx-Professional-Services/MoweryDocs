# Service Call (Mobile App Walkthrough)

## Overview

The **Service Call** screen is the first screen you see when you log in to the mobile app. It serves as your main workspace for viewing and managing service calls.

This guide walks you through the full workflow, from reviewing a service call to creating estimates, invoicing, and recording payments.

---

## Step 1: Review the Service Call

When the screen loads, the **Ticket Details** tab is selected by default.

Start by reviewing the key information:

### Check the Schedule

- **Schedule Date** – Confirms the service date.
    
- **Time Slot** – Indicates whether the job is in the AM or PM.
    

### Confirm Customer Details

- **Customer Name and ID** appear on the left panel.
    
- Review the **Contact Info** section for phone numbers.
    

### Verify the Service Location

- Check the **Property Address** to ensure you are going to the correct site.
    

---

## Step 2: Understand the Job

Scroll to the **Description** field and read the full job details carefully.

Look for:

- Type of service (e.g., tune-up, repair)
    
- Agreement details and expiration dates
    
- Special instructions
    

> **Tip:** Always review the Description before starting work to avoid missing details.

---

## Step 3: Review Service Information

Check the service configuration:

- **Service Department** – Identifies the assigned team
    
- **Service Type** – Defines the job category
    
- **Payment Type** – Indicates how the job will be paid (e.g., prepaid)
    

---

## Step 4: Check Additional Instructions


---

## Step 5: Navigate Between Tabs

Use the tabs at the top of the screen to access more information:

### Ticket Details (Default View)

- Contains all core job information
    

### Service Call History

- View previous service visits
    
- Helps understand past issues or recurring problems
    

### Equipment / Maintenance Agreements

- Check installed equipment
    
- Review active service agreements
    

### Estimate

- View or create job estimates (see Steps 8–10 below)
    

### Invoice

- Access billing details and payment records (see Steps 11–15 below)
    

---

## Step 6: Refresh the Data

Tap **Refresh** in the header to reload the service call.

Use this when:

- You expect updates from dispatch
    
- Changes were made to the job details
    

---

## Step 7: Close the App

Tap **Close App** when you are done using the mobile app.

---

# Estimate → Invoice Workflow

After reviewing the service call, you can create an **Estimate** and convert it into an **Invoice**.

---

## Step 8: Open the Estimate Tab

1. Tap the **Estimate** tab at the top of the screen.
    
2. The screen displays any existing estimates for the service call.
    

---

## Step 9: Review or Create an Estimate

### If an Estimate Exists

- Review listed items and pricing.
    
- Confirm quantities and services match the work required.
    

### If No Estimate Exists

1. Add line items for:
    
    - Services performed
        
    - Parts used
        
2. Ensure all required items are included before proceeding.
    

> **Tip:** Verify accuracy before moving to invoicing.

---

## Step 10: Verify Estimate Details

Before converting the estimate:

- Confirm pricing and totals
    
- Check applicable taxes
    
- Ensure all services and materials are listed
    
- Review with the customer if required
    

---

## Step 11: Convert Estimate to Invoice

1. Navigate to the **Invoice** tab.
    
2. Create or open the invoice linked to the estimate.
    
3. In most cases, the invoice will automatically include all items from the estimate.
    

---

## Step 12: Review the Invoice

- Confirm all estimate items are included.
    
- Verify:
    
    - Total amount
        
    - Taxes
        
    - Payment type
        

> **Tip:** Ensure everything matches the agreed work.

---

## Step 13: Record Payment

If payment is collected on-site:

1. Confirm the **Payment Type** (e.g., prepaid, card, cash)
    
2. Record the payment according to your workflow
    
3. Ensure payment details are accurate before submission
    

---

## Step 14: Finalize the Invoice

1. Mark the invoice as **Completed** or **Processed** (depending on system setup)
    
2. Ensure the service call is ready for closing
    

---

## Step 15: Verify Completion

Before leaving the job:

- Recheck the **Invoice** tab
    
- Confirm:
    
    - Invoice is created
        
    - Payment is recorded (if applicable)
        
    - No missing items
        

> **Tip:** Completing these checks reduces billing errors and ensures accurate records.

# Checklist Tab – View Checklist

## Overview

The **View Checklist** feature generates a detailed service checklist for the selected job. The checklist documents inspection results, equipment information, and service measurements performed during a maintenance visit.

Technicians can use this document to verify that all required inspection tasks are completed and to record operational readings.

---

# Generate the checklist

1. Open the **Checklist** tab for the selected job.
    
2. Click **View Checklist** in the **upper-right corner** of the screen.
    

The system generates a checklist document that includes job details, equipment information, and inspection results.

---

# Checklist layout

The generated checklist contains the following sections:

- Job and customer information
    
- Equipment information
    
- Plumbing checklist
    
- HVAC checklist
    

---

# Job and customer information

The header displays job and customer details associated with the service visit.

Fields may include:

- Customer name
    
- Work phone
    
- Home phone
    
- Mobile phone
    
- Email address
    
- Property address
    
- Billing address
    
- Service type
    
- Estimated start date
    
- Estimated completion date
    
- Invoice date
    
- Due date
    
- Invoice number
    

This information helps verify the service location and customer record.

---

# Equipment section

The **Equipment** table lists the equipment serviced during the visit.

Columns include:

- **Equipment Type**
    
- **Model #**
    
- **Serial #**
    
- **Age**
    
- **Condition**

______
## Sign and Send the Invoice

After generating the invoice, sign and email it to the customer.

## Steps

1. Tap **Generate Invoice**.
    
2. Enter the **Technician Name** and **Customer Name**, when signing an invoice.
    
3. Add the **Technician Signature** and **Customer Signature** in the fields below the invoice. You can have the option to email the invoice immediately after completing the signatures.
    
4. Resend the invoice by clicking **Email PDF.**
        
5. The system will then check if there is an existing customer email. 
    
Technicians should verify that equipment information matches the installed unit.

# Plumbing checklist

The **Plumbing** section includes inspection and maintenance tasks related to water systems, water heaters, and pumps.

Technicians select the checkbox next to each completed task and record measurements when required.

## Operational checks

- Test Operation
    
- Drain & Flush Water Heater
    
- Test Thermocouple ______ mV
    
- Vacuum Combustion Air Inlet
    
- Inspect Flue Pipe @ Water Heater
    
- Test Heating Elements ______ OHMS
    
- Check Electrical Connections
    
- Test Home’s Main Water Pressure ______ PSI
    

## Tankless water heater maintenance

These items apply to tankless systems.

- Circulate Descaler (Tankless)
    
- Clean Air Inlet Screen (Tankless)
    

## Water hardness measurements

Technicians record water hardness readings in **grains per gallon (GPG)**.

- Incoming Water ______ GPG
    
- Outlet Water ______ GPG
    

## System inspection

Inspect the system for proper installation and operation.

- Inspect For Water Leaks At Unit
    
- Properly Grounded
    
- Inspect Piping @ Pump
    

## Pump and pit maintenance

These tasks apply to sump pumps or pit systems.

- Check Float Operation & Adjustment
    
- Vacuum Pit
    
- Check Weep Hole
    
- Fill Pit & Test Operation
    
- Test Check Valve
    

---

# Best practices

- Complete checklist items during the service visit whenever possible.
    
- Record all measurements accurately.
    
- Inspect components carefully for wear, leaks, or damage.
    
- Verify system operation after completing maintenance tasks.
    
- Review the entire checklist before finalizing the job or invoice.
