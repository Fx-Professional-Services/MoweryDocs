
# Invoices

## Overview

The **Invoice List** screen displays all invoices in the system.  
Use this screen to:

- View invoices and their payment status
    
- Search for specific invoices
    
- Open an invoice to view details
    

Each row in the table represents a single invoice.

---

# Open the Invoice List

1. From the main navigation menu, click **Invoices**.
    

The **Invoice List** screen opens and displays all available invoices.

---

# Search for an Invoice

Use the **Search** field at the top of the screen to find invoices.

1. Click the **Search** field.
    
2. Enter an invoice number or customer name.
    
3. Press **Enter**.
    

The table updates to show matching results.

To clear the search, remove the text from the search field.

---

# Invoice Table

The invoice table lists invoices with key information.

|Column|Description|
|---|---|
|**Invoice Number**|Unique identifier for the invoice.|
|**Date**|Date the invoice was created.|
|**Due Date**|Date payment is due.|
|**Customer**|Customer associated with the invoice.|
|**Status**|Current payment status of the invoice.|

---

# Invoice Status Types

The **Status** column indicates whether an invoice has been paid.

|Status|Description|
|---|---|
|**Open**|The invoice has not been paid.|
|**Partially Paid**|A portion of the invoice amount has been paid.|
|**Paid**|The invoice has been fully paid.|

---

# View Invoice Details

To open a specific invoice:

1. Locate the invoice in the table.
    
2. Click the row containing the invoice number.
    

The system opens the **Invoice Details** screen for that invoice.

# Invoice Screen – How to Use It

## Purpose

Use the **Invoice Screen** to create and manage invoices for completed service work. The screen allows you to:

- Record customer and property details
    
- Add service tasks performed
    
- Apply service agreement pricing
    
- Calculate tax and total charges
    
- Track invoice status and payment
    

---

# Typical Workflow

Most users complete invoices in the following order:

1. Locate or create an invoice
    
2. Verify customer information
    
3. Confirm service location
    
4. Add service tasks
    
5. Review pricing and totals
    
6. Save and finalize the invoice
    

---

# 1. Locate or Open an Invoice

Use the search tools at the top of the screen.

### To find an invoice

1. Enter an invoice number or keyword in **QuickFind Search**.
    
2. Press **Enter**.
    
3. Review the invoice that appears.
    

### To browse invoices

Use the navigation buttons in the upper-right corner:

- First record
    
- Previous record
    
- Next record
    
- Last record
    

These controls allow you to move through stored invoices.

---

# 2. Verify Customer Information

Before adding charges, confirm the customer details.

Check the following fields:

- Customer name
    
- Email address
    
- Phone numbers
    

### Why this matters

Correct contact information ensures the invoice can be delivered and payment issues can be resolved quickly.

Update any incorrect information before continuing.

---

# 3. Confirm the Service Location

Verify the **Property Address** section.

This address identifies where the service was performed.

Common scenarios include:

- The customer lives at the service address.
    
- The service occurred at a rental property.
    
- The billing address is different from the property address.
    

Ensure the correct address is recorded before invoicing.

---

# 4. Confirm the Billing Address

Check where the invoice should be sent.

The billing address may differ from the service location. For example:

- Property managers
    
- Landlords
    
- Corporate billing departments
    

Update the billing address if necessary.

---

# 5. Add Service Tasks

The **Service Tasks Table** is where you record work performed.

Each row represents a service item.

### To add a service task

1. Click an empty row in the table.
    
2. Enter the **Task Code**.
    
3. Verify the **Description**.
    
4. Enter the **Quantity**.
    
5. Confirm the pricing fields.
    

The system automatically calculates:

- Line totals
    
- Service agreement discounts
    

### Example

A technician installs an electronic toilet seat.

The invoice might include:

- Task code for installation
    
- Quantity of 1
    
- Standard or service agreement price
    

The total updates automatically.

---

# 6. Review Service Agreement Pricing

Check the **Service Agreement** section.

If the customer has an active agreement:

- Discounted pricing may apply.
    
- The **SA Price** column will reflect the reduced rate.
    
- The **SA Savings** column shows the amount saved.
    

Verify the agreement status before finalizing the invoice.

---

# 7. Review Notes and Internal Comments

Use the **Note** and **Memo** fields to document additional information.

### Use Notes for:

- Customer-visible details
    
- Service explanations
    
- Special instructions
    

### Use Memo for:

- Internal comments
    
- Technician notes
    
- Administrative details
    

This information helps staff understand the invoice later.

---

# 8. Review Invoice Totals

Before completing the invoice, verify the totals.

Check:

- Line item totals
    
- Tax amount
    
- Total amount due
    

The system calculates these values automatically based on the tasks entered.

---

# 9. Finalize the Invoice

After verifying all information:

1. Confirm all service tasks are listed.
    
2. Review customer and billing information.
    
3. Verify the total amount due.
    

Once finalized, the invoice can be sent to the customer or marked as paid.
