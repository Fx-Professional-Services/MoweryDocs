
# Counter Sales 
## Overview

The **Counter Sales screen** allows staff to create and manage in-store sales transactions. These transactions are recorded as **counter tickets**, which track products purchased by customers.

Use this screen to:

- Create a new counter sales ticket
    
- Search for customers
    
- Add purchased products
    
- Review previous purchases
    
- Calculate sales totals and tax
    
- Print the sales ticket
    

---

# Screen Layout

The Counter Sales screen includes the following sections:

1. Navigation and ticket controls
    
2. Customer information
    
3. Products purchased
    
4. Previous purchase history
    
5. Comments
    
6. Sales totals
    

Each section helps record and manage a customer’s purchase.

---

# 1. Create or Locate a Ticket

Use the toolbar at the top of the screen to manage sales tickets.

|Button|Purpose|
|---|---|
|**List View**|Displays a list of all counter sales tickets|
|**New Ticket**|Creates a new sales ticket|
|**Find All**|Shows all sales records|
|**Print**|Prints the current sales ticket|

### QuickFind Search

Use **QuickFind Search** to locate an existing ticket.

Steps:

1. Click inside the **QuickFind Search** field.
    
2. Enter a ticket number or customer name.
    
3. Press **Enter**.
    

The matching ticket record will open.

---

# 2. Enter Customer Information

The **Customer Information** section records who made the purchase.

|Field|Description|
|---|---|
|**Customer #**|Unique identifier for the customer|
|**Company**|Customer company name (if applicable)|
|**Sold To**|Name of the purchasing customer|
|**Address**|Customer street address|
|**City / State / Zip**|Customer location|
|**Email**|Customer email address|
|**Phone**|Customer phone number|
|**Tax Exempt**|Indicates whether the customer is exempt from sales tax|

### To search for a customer

1. Click **Customer Search**.
    
2. Locate the customer in the system.
    
3. Select the customer to populate the fields automatically.
    

---

# 3. Add Products to the Ticket

The **Products Purchased** table records all items bought during the sale.

Each row represents one product.

|Column|Description|
|---|---|
|**QTY**|Quantity purchased|
|**Part #**|Product part number|
|**Product Description**|Description of the product|
|**Unit**|Unit type|
|**Unit Price**|Price per item|
|**Extended Amount**|Total price for the item|

### To add a product

1. Click an empty row in the **Products Purchased** table.
    
2. Enter the **quantity**.
    
3. Enter the **part number** or product name.
    
4. Confirm the **unit price**.
    
5. Review the **extended amount**.
    

The system automatically calculates the total for each item.

---

# 4. Review Previous Purchase History

The **Previous Purchase History** section shows past transactions for the customer.

Columns include:

|Column|Description|
|---|---|
|**Date**|Date of previous purchase|
|**Ticket #**|Ticket number of the transaction|
|**Sold To**|Customer name|
|**Total**|Total purchase amount|

### Why this helps

Staff can quickly:

- Review previous orders
    
- Identify repeat purchases
    
- Verify product history
    

This is useful when customers reorder the same items.

---

# 5. Add Comments

The **Comments** section allows staff to record additional information about the sale.

Examples include:

- Special order notes
    
- Customer requests
    
- Payment details
    
- Delivery instructions
    

Keep comments short and clear so other staff members can understand the transaction.

---

# 6. Review Sales Totals

The **Totals** area calculates the final cost of the transaction.

|Field|Description|
|---|---|
|**Subtotal**|Total cost of items before tax|
|**Tax**|Sales tax applied to the purchase|
|**Shipping**|Shipping charges if applicable|
|**Total**|Final amount due|

The system updates these values automatically when products are added or edited.

---

# 7. Print the Sales Ticket

After reviewing the transaction:

1. Verify the customer information.
    
2. Confirm the purchased items and quantities.
    
3. Review the total amount due.
    
4. Click **Print**.
    

The system prints the counter sales ticket for the customer.

---

# 8. Record Tracking Information

The bottom of the screen tracks system activity.

|Field|Description|
|---|---|
|**Created By**|User who created the ticket|
|**Date Created**|When the ticket was created|
|**Modified By**|User who last edited the ticket|
|**Date Modified**|When the ticket was last updated|

This information helps track changes to sales records.

---

# Best Practices

### Verify customer tax status

Confirm whether the customer is **tax exempt** before completing the sale.

### Check product quantities

Ensure quantities and prices are correct before printing the ticket.

### Use purchase history

Review previous purchases to help customers reorder items quickly.
