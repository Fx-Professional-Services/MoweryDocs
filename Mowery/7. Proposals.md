
## Overview

This guide explains how to create, review, and manage **Proposals** in Mowery. Proposals are used to document customer information, job details, materials, pricing, and the final scope of work before or after a sale.

---

## Before You Begin

Make sure you have:

* Customer contact and address details
* Job type and equipment information
* Material list for the installation or repair
* Pricing and install date (if sold)

---

## Proposal Screen Layout

The Proposal screen is divided into five main sections:

1. Top Navigation Bar
2. Customer Information
3. Quote Setup
4. Proposal Setup (Specifications)
5. Material List Setup

---

## Top Navigation Bar

Use the buttons at the top of the screen to manage proposals.

### Common Actions

* **Start New Proposal**: Create a new proposal record.
* **Counter Sales**: Create a counter sale instead of a proposal.
* **Sales by Tech**: View proposals by technician.
* **Find / Find All**: Search for existing proposals.
* **Delete**: Delete the current proposal.
* **Print Folder**: Print all documents tied to the proposal.
* **QuickFind Customer Search**: Search customers by phone number (area code required).

---
## Customer Information

Use this section to confirm who the proposal is for.

### Customer Fields

* **Company/Last Name** and **First Name**: Customer name.
* **Customer ID**: Auto-generated by the system.
* **Customer Email** and **Phone**: Used for proposal delivery and contact.

### Billing Address

Enter or verify:

* Street address
* City, state, and ZIP code
* Customer notes (optional)

### Location

* Enter the service location if different from billing.
* Select **Same As Billing** to copy billing details automatically.

---

## Quote Setup

Use **Quote Setup** to define the job and sales status.

### Key Fields

* **Quote Number**: Auto-assigned by the system.
* **Status**: Shows proposal state (for example, Open or Sold).
* **Type Job**: Select the job type (for example, Residential New, Commercial New, Commercial Replacement, Residential Replacement, Service Replacement, Service T&M, ).
* **Type Equip**: Select the equipment type (AC, Oil Furnace, etc.).
* **Tons of Cooling**: Enter system size.
* **Number of Furnaces**: Enter quantity if applicable.
* **Labor Hours**: Enter estimated labor time.
* **Job Finished**: Select **Yes** only after work is completed.

### Sales Details

* **Quote Date**: Date the proposal was created.
* **Salesman** and **Quoted By**: Assigned salesperson.
* **Lead Source**: How the customer was acquired.
* **Due Date**: Proposal expiration date.
* **Remarks**: Internal notes (for example, install date).

---

## Proposal Setup (Specifications)

Use this section to define what the customer is purchasing.

### Specifications Area

* Enter a clear, itemized description of the work.
* List major equipment, accessories, and services.
* Include warranty information and exclusions.

### Best Practices

* Use numbered lists for equipment and steps.
* Avoid abbreviations the customer may not understand.
* Clearly state what is included in the price.

---

## Material List Setup

Use **Material List Setup** to track all materials required for the job.

### Add Materials

1. Select **Add Row**.
2. Enter the quantity, item code, and description.
3. Repeat for each material item.

### Manage Materials

* Use the trash icon to remove a single item.
* Use **Delete Selections** to remove multiple items.
* Select **Print** to generate a material list for technicians or warehouse staff.

---

## Pricing and Review

* Review the total price displayed in the proposal.
* Confirm materials and specifications match the quoted amount.
* Verify install date and job details before finalizing.

---
## Financial Summary and Agreement Details

Use this section at the bottom of the proposal to confirm totals, payment terms, and responsible contacts.

### Pricing Summary

- **Total Options $**: Displays the total price of all selected options.
    
- **Total Estimate $**: Displays the final estimated cost for the proposal.
    

Verify these totals match the pricing shown in the Specifications section before sending the proposal.

### Field Engineer / Consultant

- **Name**: Assigned field engineer or consultant for the job.
    
- **Email** and **Phone**: Contact details shown on the proposal for customer reference.
    

Ensure this information is accurate, as it appears on printed and emailed proposals.

### Preventive Maintenance Agreement

- Use this field to note any preventive maintenance agreement included with the proposal.
    
- Enter the number of **years** and **maintenance visits per year**, if applicable.
    

Leave this blank if no maintenance agreement is included.

### Payment Terms

- Review the **Payment to be made as follows** field.
    
- Common entry: `Payment upon completion`.
    

Make sure payment terms are clear and approved before marking the proposal as sold.

_________________

## Final Actions

Use the buttons at the bottom of the screen to complete the workflow:

* **Clear**: Reset the proposal form.
* **Email**: Send the proposal to the customer.
* **Print**: Print a copy for records or customer signature.

---

## After the Proposal Is Sold

When a proposal is marked as **Sold**:

* Confirm install date in Remarks.
* Ensure the material list is complete.
* Verify pricing and labor hours.
* Leave **Job Finished** set to **No** until work is complete.

---

## Troubleshooting

**Customer information is incorrect**
Update the customer record before printing or emailing the proposal.

**Materials missing from install**
Review and complete the Material List Setup before the install date.

**Proposal not found**
Use **Find All** or search by customer phone number.
