# 📦 View Product List and Details

This guide explains how to view the list of products in the system and access detailed information for each one.

---

## 📋 Open the Product List

1. From the main menu, go to **System Defaults → Products**. The **Product List** screen appears, showing all available products.

---

## 🔍 View a Product’s Details
2. Locate the product you want to review. 
3. Click the leftmost button in the product row to open the **Product Detail** page.

## 🧾 Understand the Product Detail Sections

Each product has multiple sections that provide specific information.
### 🔹 I.  Product Info

This section provides general information that helps classify and describe the product.

- **Type** – The high-level classification of the product, typically based on trade or service area.  
  _Example: HVAC, Plumbing

- **Category** – A mid-level grouping that describes the general function or use of the product within its type.  
  _Example: Refrigerant Repairs, Drain Cleaning

- **Subcategory** – A more specific breakdown of the category, often identifying a particular variant or application.  
  _Example: Refrigerant Repairs – R410A, Drain Cleaning – Kitchen Sink, Electrical Diagnostics – Panel Issues_

- **Description** – A short summary of the product or service that explains what it is and when it is used.

- **Savings** – Any predefined value-based advantage this product provides (e.g., bundled discount, time savings).

- **Active** – Indicates whether the product is currently available for use. A checked box means it is active.

> **Purpose:** This section ensures each product is correctly classified and described, making it easier for teams to find, understand, and use the right item during sales or service workflows.
---

### 🔹 II. Product Price Attributes

Provides details about how the product’s price is structured:

- **Cost** – The base cost of the product
- **Estimated Hours** – Estimated labor time to deliver the product or service
- **Rates**:
  - **Add-On** – Additional rate when the product is added to another job
  - **Commercial** – Rate applied for commercial jobs

> **Purpose**: Helps calculate pricing based on different job types and conditions.

---

### 🔹 III. Current Pricing

Shows the product’s price as of the current or most recent effective date:

- **Revision** – Version of the pricing setup
- **Effective Date** – Date the pricing went into effect
- **Standard** – Base retail price
- **Value** – Discounted or adjusted rate
- **Commercial** – Rate for commercial applications
- **Add-On Standard** – Add-on base price
- **Add-On Value** – Discounted add-on price

> **Purpose**: Allows users to see what customers are charged under various pricing structures.

---

### 🔹 IV. Pricing Tracking and History

Located in the upper-right corner, this section shows a log of all pricing changes over time.

> **Purpose**: Helps track pricing adjustments, view historical rates, and manage version control of product pricing.

---

## ⚙️ V. Developer Settings Tip

> In developer settings, you can enable auto-entry of **Add-On** and **Commercial** rates when creating new products.  
> This setting applies when you click the **New Product** button.

##  VI.  Category and Subcategory

**Category**: A top-level group used to organize service calls, identified by a name and a type (`HVAC` or `Plumbing`). Categories can share names if their types differ.

**Subcategory**: A specific group linked to a category. Subcategories inherit the type from their parent category and are required for product assignment.
